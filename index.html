Mortgage glossary
Every key term explained in plain English
Amortisation
The gradual repayment of your mortgage through monthly payments. Each covers interest and capital, with the split slowly shifting in your favour over 30 years.
APRC
Your APRC is 5.9%. A standardised figure showing total annual cost including fees and the variable rate period — used to compare mortgages fairly.
Bank of England Base Rate
The rate set by the Bank of England that influences mortgage rates. Your fixed rate of 4.53% is locked regardless of base rate changes until 28/02/2031.
Capital
The actual sum borrowed — £296,995. Each payment reduces this. Overpayments reduce it faster. Every pound of capital reduction permanently reduces future interest.
Early Repayment Charge (ERC)
A penalty if you repay beyond your 10% annual allowance before the fix ends. Starts at 6% and reduces each year. Compensates Skipton for fixing your rate.
Fixed rate
Your rate of 4.53% is locked until 28/02/2031 regardless of Bank of England changes — up or down.
Interest
The charge Skipton applies for lending the money. Month 1: approximately £1,121. Falls as your balance reduces — every pound of capital reduction saves future interest.
LTV (Loan to Value)
Balance ÷ property value. You started at 90%. Lower LTV = better rates at remortgage. Crossing from 90% to 85% to 80% unlocks progressively better deals.
Overpayment
Any payment above your standard £1,508.84/month. Up to 10% of outstanding balance per year without ERC. Goes directly to capital — immediately reduces future interest.
Payment holiday
A temporary pause available after 6 months with overpayment surplus. Up to 3 consecutive months. Interest still accrues — balance may increase slightly.
Portability
Your mortgage can transfer to a new property without triggering the ERC, provided both transactions complete within 6 months of each other.
Product Transfer
Switching to a new Skipton deal at the end of the fix without leaving Skipton. Fast and simple. Always compare with the wider market first.
Remortgage
Moving to a completely new lender at the end of the fix. Full application, solicitor required, 4 to 8 weeks. Access to the whole market for potentially better rates.
SVR (Standard Variable Rate)
Skipton's default rate of 6.54%, applied automatically if you do nothing when the fix expires on 28/02/2031. Payment jumps to £1,832.08. Never stay on the SVR.

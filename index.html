Where does £1,508.84 go?
Every payment is split between two things
Each payment goes to interest (cost of borrowing) and capital (reducing your debt). At the start the split heavily favours interest.
In month 1, roughly £1,121 goes to Skipton as interest. Only £388 reduces your actual debt. That is 74% to interest and just 26% to capital.
This is called amortisation. On a 30 year term this shift is painfully slow without overpayments.

Mortgage glossary
Key terms explained in plain English
Amortisation
The process of gradually paying off your mortgage. Each payment covers interest and capital, with the split shifting over time so more goes to capital as the balance falls.
APRC (Annual Percentage Rate of Charge)
Your APRC is 5.9%. A standardised figure representing the total annual cost including fees and the variable rate period — used to compare mortgage products fairly.
Capital
The actual amount borrowed — £296,995. Each payment reduces this slightly. Overpayments reduce it faster. The faster it falls, the less interest you pay.
Early Repayment Charge (ERC)
A penalty charged if you repay beyond your 10% allowance before the fixed rate ends. Your ERC starts at 6% and reduces each year to compensate Skipton for fixing your rate.
Fixed rate
Your rate of 4.53% is guaranteed until 28/02/2031 regardless of Bank of England base rate changes. This gives certainty but limits switching without an ERC.
Interest
The cost Skipton charges for lending the money. Calculated on your outstanding balance. In month 1 this is approximately £1,121. As your balance falls, so does the interest charge.
LTV (Loan to Value)
Your mortgage balance divided by your property value. You started at 90% (£296,995 ÷ £329,995). Lower LTV = better rates at remortgage. Every £1 of capital repaid improves your LTV.
Overpayment
Any payment above your standard monthly amount. Your mortgage allows up to 10% of your outstanding balance per year without ERC. Overpayments go directly to capital.
Payment holiday
A temporary pause in payments available if you have built up overpayment surplus. Up to 3 consecutive months. Interest still accrues — your balance may increase slightly.
Portability
Your mortgage can transfer to a new property without triggering the ERC, provided both transactions complete simultaneously or within 6 months of each other.
SVR (Standard Variable Rate)
Skipton's default rate of 6.54%, applied automatically if you do nothing when your fixed rate expires. Your payment would jump to £1,832.08. Always remortgage before reaching this point.
